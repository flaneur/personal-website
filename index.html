He Zhao 赵鹤

I am a master's student in Ethnology at Minzu University of China. My work engages historical sociology, cultural sociology, political sociology, sociology of the arts, memory studies, social movements, political economy, social theory, and computational social science.

I received my Bachelor of Laws in Sociology from Minzu University of China, with minors in Anthropology and Archaeology. Methodologically, I work with comparative historical methods, ethnography, oral history, machine learning, computational text analysis, and bibliometric analysis.

PUBLICATIONS

The Mental Structures and the “Class” Tastes Behind the Reinventing Historical Imagination

ZHAO He. 2026. “The Mental Structures and the ‘Class’ Tastes Behind the Reinventing Historical Imagination: The Adaptation of the Peking Opera The Lucky Purse During the ‘Xiqu Reform’ in the 1950s” [重塑历史想象中的心智结构与“阶级”趣味——20世纪50年代“戏改”期间京剧《锁麟囊》的改编]. Open Times 45(3).

This article studies the adaptation of the Peking Opera The Lucky Purse during the “Xiqu Reform” of the 1950s, focusing on historical imagination, mental structures, and the political-cultural reconstruction of artistic taste.

Peasant Action, Agency-Social Structure, and the Construction of Specialty Industries

ZHAO He. 2025. “Peasant Action and Agency-Social Structure and the Construction of Specialty Industries: A Case Study of the Developmental Process of ‘Wuchang Rice’” [农民行动、能动性—社会结构与特色产业建构——“五常大米”发展过程的个案研究]. Rural China: An International Journal of History and Social Science 22(1): 93–130. DOI: 10.1163/22136746-12341327.

This study examines the developmental process of Wuchang Rice and analyzes the interaction between peasant agency, social structure, local government, agricultural science and technology, and market formation.

RESEARCH PROJECTS

Examining Livelihood Practices, Relational Structures, and the Localization of Political Culture

Graduate Research Project of Minzu University of China, 2025–2027. Supervisors: Zhang Liang and Li Kang.

This independent master's thesis project develops a social history of a Northeast Chinese village from 1930 to 2012. It uses cultural sociology and anthropological methodologies to analyze livelihood practices, relational structures, and political culture.

Rice’s Wisdom: Social Interaction, Farmers’ Subjectivity, and Specialty Industry Formation

Special Research Project at Minzu University of China, 2022–2023. Supervisors: Li Kang and Zhang Liang.

This project combined archival analysis, oral history, and ethnographic writing to study how the Wuchang Rice industry was shaped by national politics, economic environments, grassroots government, agricultural science and technology, and farmers’ subjectivity.

Research on the Diversity of China’s Hospice Care Models

Tsinghua University Independent Research Program, 2022–2023. Supervisors: Jing Jun and Sun Weiwei.

The project involved household interviews and narrative studies related to care, aging, death, and the diversity of hospice care models in China.
